Abstract

This survey project investigated public awareness and perception of pollution and its impacts. The findings show that respondents are aware of pollution's harmful effects on the environment and human health, and they believe it's a significant problem in their community.

  • Most respondents identify air and land pollution as major environmental causes.
  • Many report experiencing health problems they attribute to pollution.
  • Despite awareness, there is a gap between knowledge and action, though a majority of people are calling for greater personal accountability to address the issue.

Introduction

Pollution is a growing global concern, posing significant risks to human health, the environment, and the economy. The World Health Organization (WHO) estimates that pollution is responsible for approximately 9 million premature deaths annually, with air pollution being the largest environmental health risk.

Types of Pollution

  • Air pollution: From industrial activities, vehicle exhaust, and fossil fuels.
  • Water pollution: From industrial waste, agricultural runoff, and human activities.
  • Land pollution: Contamination from industrial waste and agricultural chemicals.
  • Noise pollution: Excessive noise from transportation and industry.

Impacts of Pollution

  • Human Health: Respiratory problems, cardiovascular diseases, and cancer.
  • Environmental Degradation: Climate change, deforestation, and loss of biodiversity.
  • Economic Costs: Healthcare expenses and lost productivity.

Methodology

This quantitative study used an online survey questionnaire distributed via social media platforms to a target population of 32 university students. The data was analyzed using Jupiter notebook, employing descriptive and inferential statistics. A stratified random sampling technique was used to select respondents from different genders and departments to ensure data quality.

Key Findings

  • Concern about Pollution: A significant majority of participants expressed concern about pollution in their area. The bar chart for Question 1 shows that both males and females are primarily "Somewhat concerned".

    Responses to Q1: How concerned are you about pollution in your area?

  • Most Concerning Type: Most participants identified air pollution and land pollution as the most concerning types globally.

    Responses to Q2: In your opinion, which type of pollution is the most concerning globally?

  • Responsibility: The majority of respondents believe that individuals have the biggest responsibility to address pollution.

    Responses to Q4: In your opinion, who has the biggest responsibility to address pollution?

  • Willingness to Participate: Both male and female participants expressed a willingness to participate in activities that reduce pollution, indicating a positive attitude towards taking action.

    Responses to Q5: How likely are you to participate in activities that reduce pollution?

Conclusion

The analysis yielded mixed results. We were unable to reject the null hypothesis for four of the five hypotheses, suggesting no statistically significant evidence to disprove the initial claims in those areas. However, one hypothesis did provide sufficient evidence to reject the null hypothesis. The results confirmed a general awareness and concern about pollution, with most people believing that individuals hold the primary responsibility to act.
